Other Applications of Ultrasonic sound
(Physics Presentation-Roll no 191 to 199)

Ultrasonic sound is sound waves with frequency higher than the audible frit of human hearing, storting from frequencies approximately 20 kHz up to several gigahertzes.

It works by transmitting sound pulses through the body and detecting the returning echoes.

Ultrasonic sounds produce the types of vibrating systems:

  1. longitudinal vibrating system
  2. Torsional vibrating system
  3. Transverse vibrating system.

Longitudinal vibrating system

Longitudinal vibrating system is used in many fields, e.g. for welding plastics and non-ferrous metals or for cleaning

Torsional vibrating system

Torsional vibrating system is used to characterize the biomechanics of soft tissues

Transverse vibrating system

Transverse vibrating system is used for exciting screens.
